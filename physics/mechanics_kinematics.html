Kinematics (Motion in a Straight Line)

This section covers the basic equations describing motion in a straight line under constant acceleration.

Velocity-Time Relation (Constant Acceleration)

\(v = v_0 + a t\)

Variables:

  • \(v\): final velocity.
  • \(v_0\): initial velocity.
  • \(a\): constant acceleration.
  • \(t\): time interval.

Description/Usage:

Calculates the final velocity of an object after a certain time, given its initial velocity and constant acceleration. This is one of the fundamental equations for uniformly accelerated linear motion.

Visual: Velocity-time graph (straight line).

Displacement-Time Relation (Constant Acceleration)

\(\Delta x = v_0 t + \tfrac{1}{2}a t^2\)

Variables:

  • \(\Delta x\): displacement (change in position).
  • \(v_0\): initial velocity.
  • \(t\): time interval.
  • \(a\): constant acceleration.

Description/Usage:

Calculates the displacement of an object after a certain time, given its initial velocity and constant acceleration. Useful for determining how far an object travels.

Visual: Displacement-time graph (parabola).

Velocity-Displacement Relation (Constant Acceleration)

\(v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x\)

Variables:

  • \(v\): final velocity.
  • \(v_0\): initial velocity.
  • \(a\): constant acceleration.
  • \(\Delta x\): displacement.

Description/Usage:

Relates final velocity to initial velocity, acceleration, and displacement, without directly involving time. This is particularly useful when time is not known or not required.

Visual: Object accelerating along a path.

Projectile Range (level ground)

\(R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g}\)

Variables:

  • \(R\): horizontal range.
  • \(v_0\): initial launch speed.
  • \(\theta\): launch angle above horizontal.
  • \(g\): acceleration due to gravity (\(\approx9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\) downward).

Description/Usage:

Gives the horizontal distance (range) of a projectile launched on level ground (initial and final heights equal) neglecting air resistance. Max range occurs at \(\theta=45^\circ\).

Visual: Parabolic trajectory of a projectile.
