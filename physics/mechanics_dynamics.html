Dynamics (Forces & Newton's Laws)

This section deals with the effect of forces on the motion of objects, based on Newton's Laws.

Newton's Second Law

\(\mathbf{F}_{\text{net}} = m \mathbf{a}\)

Variables:

  • \(m\): mass of an object.
  • \(\mathbf{a}\): acceleration vector of the object.
  • \(\mathbf{F}_{\text{net}}\): net force vector acting on the object.

Description/Usage:

Fundamental law relating force, mass, and acceleration. It implies acceleration is proportional to net force and inversely proportional to mass.

Visual: Free-body diagram and resulting acceleration.

Weight (Force due to Gravity)

\(W = m g\)

Variables:

  • \(W\): weight (force).
  • \(m\): mass.
  • \(g\): acceleration due to gravity (on Earth, \(g \approx 9.8\,\text{m/s}^2\) downward).

Description/Usage:

Weight is the gravitational force exerted on a mass by Earth (or another celestial body). It differs from mass; mass is constant, weight depends on local \(g\).

Visual: Spring scale comparison on Earth vs. Moon.

Momentum & Impulse

\(\mathbf{p} = m \mathbf{v}\)      \(\mathbf{J} = \Delta \mathbf{p} = \mathbf{F}_{\text{avg}}\,\Delta t\)

Variables:

  • \(\mathbf{p}\): momentum vector.
  • \(m\): mass.
  • \(\mathbf{v}\): velocity.
  • \(\mathbf{J}\): impulse (vector).
  • \(\Delta \mathbf{p}\): change in momentum.
  • \(\mathbf{F}_{\text{avg}}\): average force applied.
  • \(\Delta t\): time interval of force application.

Description/Usage:

Momentum is the quantity of motion. Impulse is the effect of a force acting over time, equal to the change in momentum.
