Algebra

This section covers key algebraic formulas, including solutions to equations, expansions, series sums, and factoring identities.

Quadratic Formula

\(x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\)

Variables:

  • \(a, b, c\): coefficients of a quadratic equation \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) (with \(a\neq 0\)).
  • \(x\): represents the solutions (roots) of the equation.

Description/Usage:

Gives the solutions for a quadratic equation of any form. It is typically illustrated by the parabola of \(y = ax^2+bx+c\) intersecting the \(x\)-axis at \(x\) values given by this formula (the roots).

Visual: Parabola intersecting x-axis.

Binomial Theorem

\((a + b)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n}\binom{n}{k} a^{n-k} b^k\)

Variables:

  • \(n\): a non-negative integer.
  • \(a, b\): terms of the binomial.
  • \(\binom{n}{k} = \frac{n!}{k!(n-k)!}\): the binomial coefficient.

Description/Usage:

Expands a binomial raised to an integer power into a sum of terms. Often visualized using Pascal's Triangle for binomial coefficients.

Visual: Pascal's Triangle.

Sum of a Geometric Series (finite)

\(S_n = a \frac{1 - r^n}{1 - r} \quad (\text{for } r \neq 1)\)

Variables:

  • \(S_n\): sum of the first \(n\) terms.
  • \(a\): the first term of the geometric series.
  • \(r\): the common ratio.
  • \(n\): the number of terms.

Description/Usage:

Calculates the sum of the first \(n\) terms of a geometric progression. If \(|r|<1\), as \(n \to \infty\), the series converges to \(S_{\infty} = \frac{a}{1-r}\). A geometric series can be represented by successively smaller segments or areas to illustrate convergence.

Visual: Converging geometric segments.

Common Factoring Identity (Difference of Squares)

\(a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)\)

Variables:

  • \(a, b\): any expressions or numbers.

Description/Usage:

Fundamental algebraic identity used to factor the difference between two squares. It's often demonstrated graphically by the difference in areas of squares of side \(a\) and side \(b\).

Visual: Difference in areas of squares.
