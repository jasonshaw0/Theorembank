Plane Geometry

This section explores formulas related to two-dimensional shapes, their properties, areas, and perimeters.

Pythagorean Theorem

\(c^2 = a^2 + b^2\)

Variables:

  • \(a, b\): lengths of the legs (perpendicular sides) of a right triangle.
  • \(c\): length of the hypotenuse (side opposite the right angle).

Description/Usage:

Relates the side lengths of a right-angled triangle. It is used to find the third side given the other two.

Visual: Three squares on the sides of a right triangle.

Area of Common Shapes

Rectangle \(A = L \times W\); Triangle \(A = \frac{1}{2} b h\); Circle \(A = \pi r^2\); Trapezoid \(A = \frac{1}{2}(a+b)h\)

Variables:

  • \(L, W\): length and width of a rectangle.
  • \(b, h\): base and height of a triangle.
  • \(r\): radius of a circle.
  • \(a, b\): lengths of the parallel sides of a trapezoid, \(h\) is height.

Description/Usage:

Formulas for areas of basic plane figures.

Visual: Rectangle, triangle, circle, trapezoid shapes.
